Core Messaging Framework

All communications ‚Äî from a 280-character tweet to a 3-hour podcast ‚Äî must be anchored to these core messages. Repetition is the engine of credibility. Consistency is indistinguishable from conviction.

Master Narrative: The Arc of History

"Venezuela chose freedom at the ballot box. The world heard us. And now, the future we fought for is finally within reach."

This is the overarching frame. MCM is not a refugee or an exile. She is the leader of a democratic movement in the final yards of a 25-year race. Every interview should be building toward this historical destination.

Message 1: The Democratic Mandate Is Unambiguous

"The Venezuelan people voted. We have the tally sheets. Edmundo Gonz√°lez won ‚Äî and that mandate has not expired."

Always return to the documented evidence of electoral victory. This is her legal and moral anchor. It counters the Trump administration's move toward Delcy Rodr√≠guez without directly attacking Trump.

Message 2: She Is Trump's Best Bet for a Stable Venezuela

"A democratic transition in Venezuela is also a strategic victory for the United States. Stability, oil, and an end to Russian and Iranian influence ‚Äî this is what we offer."

Speak Trump's language: deals, strength, winning. Frame Venezuelan democracy not as idealism but as American strategic interest. This is the argument that moves the Trump White House.

Message 3: The Political Prisoners Must Be Released

"There are still over 800 Venezuelans imprisoned for the crime of loving their country. No transition is complete until every one of them is free."

This humanizes the cause, creates a measurable metric of progress, and gives Congress members a concrete issue to champion. It also positions MCM as morally relentless ‚Äî a Nobel Prize winner who never stops fighting for individuals.

Message 4: She Is Venezuela's Voice, Not Washington's Puppet

"I am here because the Venezuelan people sent me. I speak on their behalf ‚Äî 30 million people who deserve to determine their own future."

Critical for international credibility and long-term legitimacy. She must never appear as a U.S. proxy. This message must appear in every interview, even when thanking Trump, to preserve her independence.

Message 5: Hope and Return

"I will go back. Not as an exile returning ‚Äî but as a free Venezuelan going home."

Emotionally the most powerful message for the diaspora and for Venezuelans inside Venezuela. Her eventual return is the symbolic culmination of the entire freedom movement. Keep this front of mind in every appearance.

Language and Tone Guidelines

Always Use

  • "Democratic transition" ‚Äî not "regime change"
  • "The Venezuelan people" ‚Äî center the citizens, not herself
  • "Freedom" and "democracy" ‚Äî universal, non-partisan values
  • "Our mandate" ‚Äî inclusive, collective voice
  • Specific numbers: 800 prisoners, 7M in diaspora ‚Äî facts ground the narrative

Never Use

  • Partisan U.S. political language (don't attack Biden, Democrats, or Republicans)
  • Military or interventionist language ‚Äî she is a peace prize winner
  • "Coup" or "invasion" ‚Äî Maduro's framing; never adopt it
  • Personal attacks on Delcy Rodr√≠guez ‚Äî engage on policy, not personality
  • Premature victory declarations ‚Äî grounded, measured optimism only
